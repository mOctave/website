About Me

So who exactly am I? I'm an amateur developer and copyeditor who likes to overcomplicate everything and then pick the nits out of it. I enjoy working with a variety of tools, both writing user-facing code (although I'll never love trying to get any sort of GUI working) and developing reliable backend libraries such as Weftspace. I'm a strong believer in human-written code, strong typing or good type annotations, and open-source software.

If you're interested in what I'm doing, you can find me on GitHub or send me an email. I'm also sometimes active on the Endless Sky Discord server, so you may be able to track me down there.

One of the things I do when I have time and motivation (almost never these days, hopefully more soon™), is content review for the game Endless Sky. If you've never heard of it, I highly suggest you check it out: it was inspired by games like Escape Velocity but is very much its own game.

My Skillset

I would generally consider myself an intermediate developer according to the Developer Skills Matrix, although admittedly I still have a lot to learn in certain areas.

Over the last decade or so, I've learned how to program in about twenty different languages (most of them badly), and done several systematic development projects.
